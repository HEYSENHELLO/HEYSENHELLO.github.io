Blunvox (a Minecraft Game)

1 year ago, I was hyped to make the "Best Minecraft Game in Roblox". Fast forward to a few months ago, I got demotivated to work on Blunvox and started "Skull Simulator". The difference in thumbnail quality of Blunvox VS. Skull Simulator tells you that how much I have improved in just a few months in terms of Thumbnail Design.

What I've made in Blunvox:

Blunvox was a solo project of mine (meaning I contributed to 100% of the game) procedurally generated by advanced scripts, it doesn't involve anything related to building or modeling, although it involves texturing which were also made by me.

Below you can see descriptions to some scripting subcategories in Blunvox:

  • Terrain Generating Blunvox uses perlin noise to procedurally generate its terrains
  • Cave Generation I used 3D perlin noise to generate the caves, add ores, and much more stuff
  • Raycasting: Raycasting is an essential part of any Minecraft game, since the terrains are rendered in client-side I couldn't use Roblox's built-in raycast on the server-side, and I spent days creating my own voxel raycasting system which made me gain a lot of experience and knowledge on the topic.
  • Custom Physics: Instead of using Roblox's default physics, I implemented custom voxel physics (inspired by Minecraft's AABB or "Axis Aligned Bounding Box").
  • Texturing: I made 18x18 textures for the blocks in the game. Not 16x16 because I wanted to be different lmao
